The 17 UN Goals

The 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are a universal call to action by the United Nations, adopted in 2015 as part of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development. They aim to eradicate poverty, protect the planet, and ensure peace and prosperity for all. These goals are interdependent, addressing global challenges like hunger, education, clean water, and climate change, while promoting economic growth and reducing inequalities. Designed to inspire cooperation among nations, organizations, and individuals, the SDGs emphasize inclusivity and sustainability, striving to create a just, resilient, and thriving world for present and future generations.
